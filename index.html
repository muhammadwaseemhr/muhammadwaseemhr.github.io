What founders typically discover

A silent churn cluster

Most brands find a group of high-spending customers who bought 2–3 times then stopped — with no dashboard flag, no alert, and no follow-up sequence triggered.

→ Typically 15–25% of total customer base

Revenue concentrated in a tiny group

The top 10–15% of customers are usually generating 50–70% of total revenue. Losing even a handful of them has an outsized P&L impact that isn't visible until it's too late.

→ Identifies which relationships to protect first

A predictable buying window

Purchase frequency patterns reveal when your best customers are expected to return — and when silence means they've likely left. Most brands have no idea this window exists.

→ Turns retention from reactive to proactive
